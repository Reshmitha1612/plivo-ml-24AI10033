Our final model achieved a dev BPB of 2.1751 by maximizing parameter efficiency and sequence compression. The most critical improvement was replacing the byte-level tokenizer with a custom Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) tokenizer trained on the corpus with a vocabulary of 1000. Because Devanagari characters require three bytes, the baseline tokenizer severely crippled the 128-token context window, whereas BPE compressed the sequence by roughly 2.8x. To afford the larger embedding matrix without exceeding the 2,000,000 parameter cap, we enabled weight tying between the input and output embeddings. This strategic trade-off freed up sufficient parameter budget to widen the network's hidden dimension from 160 to 192 and increase attention heads to 6. The final architecture heavily saturates the constraints, utilizing 1,996,416 parameters (99.8% of the budget). To stabilize training over the short 2,000-step limit, we introduced gradient clipping at a norm of 1.0. Finally, we retained the baseline Adam optimizer with a constant learning rate of 3e-4, as experiments with aggressive cosine scheduling destabilized the expanded BPE embeddings. Together, these architectural and tokenization synergies perfectly exploited the strict speedrun constraints.
