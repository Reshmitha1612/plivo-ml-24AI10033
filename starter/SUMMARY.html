2. Justification of the Metric & Strategy

The baseline model struggled significantly because it utilized a byte-level tokenizer. The mixed English and Hindi dataset meant Devanagari characters were consuming 3 bytes each, artificially shrinking the model's effective context window and wasting optimization steps. By implementing a BPE Tokenizer (1000 Vocab), we compressed the dataset by ~2.8x. To afford this massive vocabulary expansion without breaching the 2M limit, we applied Weight Tying to share input/output embeddings. This freed up enough space to widen the hidden dimension to 192, vastly improving the model's representational capacity.
